Physical Constants

Constants play a crucial role in physics as they serve as fundamental building blocks for various calculations and equations across all branches of the field. Understanding and applying these constants are essential for solving problems in quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism, and other areas of physics. Here, you will find a selection of the most important physical constants, categorized by their relevant areas of application.

General Constants
  • Speed of light (c): 299,792,458 m/s (or commonly 3 x 10^8 m/s)
  • Planck's constant (h): 6.62607015 × 10^(-34) J·s
  • Boltzmann constant (k): 1.380649 × 10^(-23) J/K
  • Elementary charge (e): 1.602176634 × 10^(-19) C
  • Vacuum magnetic permeability (μ₀): 1.25663706127 × 10^(-6) N/A²
  • Vacuum electric permittivity (ε₀): 8.8541878188 × 10^(-12) F/m
  • Electron mass (mₑ): 9.10938356 × 10^(-31) kg
  • Proton mass (mₚ): 1.6726219 × 10^(-27) kg
  • Neutron mass (mₙ): 1.675 × 10^(-27) kg
Thermodynamics & Statistical Mechanics
  • Gravitational constant (G): 6.67430 × 10^(-11) m³·kg^(-1)·s^(-2)
  • Avogadro's constant (Nₐ): 6.02214076 × 10²³ mol⁻¹
  • Gas constant (R): 8.314 J/mol·K
  • Stefan-Boltzmann constant (σ): 5.670374419 × 10^(-8) W·m^(-2)·K^(-4)
Electromagnetism
  • Fine-structure constant (α): 1/137 (dimensionless)
  • Magnetic constant (μ₀/4π): 7.95774715459 × 10^(-3) N/A²
Quantum Mechanics
  • Rydberg constant (Rₑ): 1.097373 × 10⁷ m^(-1)
  • Planck length (ℓₚ): 1.616255 × 10^(-35) m
  • Atomic mass unit (u): 1.66053906660 × 10^(-27) kg
Nuclear Physics
  • Thomson scattering cross-section (σₜ): 6.652458 × 10^(-29) m²
