Physical Constants

Constants play a crucial role in physics as they serve as fundamental building blocks for various calculations and equations across all branches of the field. Understanding and applying these constants are essential for solving problems in quantum mechanics, thermodynamics, electromagnetism, and other areas of physics. Here, you will find a selection of the most important physical constants, categorized by their relevant areas of application.

General Constants
  • Speed of light (c): \( 299 792 458 \, \text{m/s} \) (or commonly \( 3 \times 10^8 \, \text{m/s} \))
  • Planck's constant (h): \( 6.62607015 \times 10^{-34} \, \text{J·s} \)
  • Boltzmann constant (k): \( 1.380649 \times 10^{-23} \, \text{J/K} \)
  • Elementary charge (e): \( 1.602176634 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{C} \)
  • Vacuum magnetic permeability (μ₀): \( 1.25663706127 \times 10^{-6} \, \text{N/A}^2 \)
  • Vacuum electric permittivity (ε₀): \( 8.8541878188 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{F/m} \)
Thermodynamics & Statistical Mechanics
  • Gravitational constant (G): \( 6.67430 \times 10^{-11} \, \text{m}^3 \cdot \text{kg}^{-1} \cdot \text{s}^{-2} \)
  • Avogadro's constant (Nₐ): \( 6.02214076 \times 10^{23} \, \text{mol}^{-1} \)
  • Gas constant (R): \( 8.314 \, \text{J/mol·K} \)
  • Stefan-Boltzmann constant (σ): \( 5.670374419 \times 10^{-8} \, \text{W} \cdot \text{m}^{-2} \cdot \text{K}^{-4} \)
Electromagnetism
  • Vacuum electric permittivity (ε₀): \( 8.8541878188 \times 10^{-12} \, \text{F/m} \)
  • Vacuum magnetic permeability (μ₀): \( 4\pi \times 10^{-7} \, \text{N/A}^2 \)
Quantum Mechanics
  • Rydberg constant (Rₑ): \( 1.097373 \times 10^7 \, \text{m}^{-1} \)
  • Planck length (ℓₚ): \( 1.616255 \times 10^{-35} \, \text{m} \)
  • Fine-structure constant (α): \( \frac{1}{137} \) (dimensionless)
Nuclear Physics
  • Electron mass (mₑ): \( 9.10938356 \times 10^{-31} \, \text{kg} \)
  • Proton mass (mₚ): \( 1.6726219 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg} \)
  • Neutron mass (mₙ): \( 1.675 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg} \)
  • Atomic mass unit (u): \( 1.66053906660 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{kg} \)
  • Thomson scattering cross-section (σₜ): \( 6.652458 \times 10^{-29} \, \text{m}^2 \)
